# Advanced Monopoly — Official Rules

Version 0.1 (Draft)

All standard Monopoly rules apply — board layout, property prices, rents, houses/hotels, mortgages (10% interest to unmortgage), jail, Chance and Community Chest, auctions, and the $200 salary for passing GO — except where modified below.

Values marked ⚙ are proposed defaults that were not explicitly decided and should be balance-tested.

---

## 1. The Card System

### 1.1 General rules

1. Cards are financial instruments created or purchased by players.
2. Creating any card costs $25, paid to the bank. This applies per card created, including each card produced when fractioning a corporation.
3. Cards may only be created on your own turn.
4. All cards are assets. Any card may be sold, traded, gifted, or pledged as loan collateral at any negotiated price.
5. Recurring obligations (loan interest, insurance premiums, corporation payouts, rebate duration) are processed when the relevant player passes GO, after collecting the $200 salary. See §7 for the exact order of operations.
6. Side deals, private negotiations, and informal agreements are always allowed. Only card terms are enforced by the game.

### 1.2 Card types

| Card | Created by | Core effect |
|---|---|---|
| Bank Loan | Bank (on request) | Borrow cash; 25% of principal due as interest each GO |
| Player Loan | Any player | Lend to another player at any negotiated rate |
| Insurance | Bank or any player | Covers large one-off expenses in $500 tranches |
| Corporation | Any property owner | Groups properties; profit shares paid to cardholders |
| Rebate | Any property owner | Fixed payout to holder when others land on listed properties |

## 2. Loans

### 2.1 Bank loans

1. Any player may borrow from the bank on their turn.
2. Borrowing limit (secured borrowing): a player's total outstanding bank principal may never exceed the total mortgage value of their unmortgaged properties. Properties already mortgaged do not count.
3. A player may hold multiple bank loans simultaneously, subject to the combined limit.
4. Interest: 25% of the original principal, due every time the borrower passes GO, for as long as any principal remains outstanding. Interest is calculated on the original amount, not the remaining balance.
5. Partial repayments may be made at any time on the borrower's turn, but the full 25%-of-original-principal interest remains due each GO until the principal is fully repaid.
6. Failure to pay interest triggers a forced asset sale (borrower must liquidate — sell houses at half price, mortgage properties, sell cards — until the interest is paid). If liquidation cannot cover it, the borrower is bankrupt.
7. If a player goes bankrupt owing the bank, the bank assumes all their assets. Properties are auctioned per standard rules; cards held by the bankrupt player are auctioned as well ⚙.

### 2.2 Player loans

1. Any player may create a Loan Card ($25) to lend cash to another player.
2. Interest rate is freely negotiable with no cap, structured the same way: the agreed percentage of the original principal is due each time the borrower passes GO until the principal is repaid.
3. Loans may be secured against specific collateral (properties or cards), named on the card at creation.
4. Default: if the borrower cannot pay interest, the lender seizes the named collateral. If the loan is unsecured or collateral is insufficient, the lender seizes other borrower assets up to the amount owed (borrower chooses which, lender may refuse manifestly unfair valuations ⚙ — resolve by auction if disputed).
5. Loan cards are tradeable. The buyer of a loan card becomes the lender and receives all future interest and repayment.
6. If a lender goes bankrupt, their loan cards transfer as assets to whoever assumes their estate (bank or seizing creditor).

## 3. Insurance

### 3.1 Coverage

1. Insurance is bought in $500 tranches. Each tranche is a per-lap coverage pool: claims draw it down, and it refills to $500 when the premium is paid at GO. A player holding 3 tranches can absorb up to $1,500 of covered losses per lap.
2. Covered events: rent, taxes, and Chance/Community Chest penalties.
3. Not covered: loan interest, insurance premiums, rebate obligations, and all voluntary spending (property purchases, buildings, card creation, trades).
4. Deductible: $100 per claim. Only single events of $200 or more are claimable.
5. Claim mechanics: the insured pays the $100 deductible plus any amount exceeding the remaining pool; the insurer pays the remainder directly to the creditor.
6. You may only insure your own expenses. Insuring another player's losses is banned.

### 3.2 Bank insurance

1. The bank sells tranches at a fixed, above-market premium: ⚙ $75 per tranche, due at each GO.
2. The bank always pays valid claims (infinite bank, see §6).

### 3.3 Player-sold insurance

1. Any player may create an Insurance Card ($25) and sell tranches at any negotiated premium.
2. Insurer default: if the insurer cannot pay a claim in full, the insured must pay the expense themselves. All premiums already paid are forfeit and the policy terminates.
3. Cancellation: either party may cancel a policy when the insured passes GO (before the premium is paid). No refunds.

## 4. Corporations

### 4.1 Formation and ownership

1. A property owner may create a Corporation Card ($25), placing one or more of their properties into the corporation. The corporation holds the properties.
2. Ownership of the corporation is expressed as profit-share percentages on corporation cards. The player holding the majority of profit share holds the title and is the controlling interest.
3. Fractioning: a cardholder may exchange their corporation card for multiple cards of smaller percentages summing to the same total ($25 per new card created). Fractions may then be sold or traded freely.

### 4.2 Profits and payouts

1. Profits = income − loan interest attributable to the corporation − rebates paid on corporate properties − improvement costs.
2. If the controlling player is also a shareholder, the corporation's accounting is fully consolidated: all income minus all expenditures, including building, distributed by share.
3. Distributions are paid out when the controlling interest passes GO (see §7).

### 4.3 Governance

1. Adding properties: the controlling interest may add their own properties to the corporation unilaterally.
2. Removing properties: requires the agreement of every cardholder of that corporation.
3. Building, mortgaging, and property decisions are made by the controlling interest. Properties inside a corporation may be mortgaged. Side deals to influence these decisions are explicitly allowed.

### 4.4 Color sets

1. If the title holder has a complete color set but loses controlling interest in any property of that set, the set bonus is suspended: no double rent on unimproved properties and no new building.
2. Existing houses and hotels remain and continue charging their built rent, but no further building is permitted until control is restored.

### 4.5 Bankruptcy

1. If the controlling interest goes bankrupt, the corporation is wiped out. All corporation cards for that corporation become void — cardholders have no claim to the properties, which pass unencumbered to the bankrupt player's creditor.
2. A minority holder's bankruptcy does not affect the corporation; their card transfers as an asset to their creditor.

## 5. Rebate Cards

1. A property owner may create a Rebate Card ($25) naming one or more of their properties and a fixed rebate amount.
2. The holder of the card receives the rebate from the property owner every time any player other than the owner lands on a listed property — even if the property is mortgaged.
3. Multiple rebate cards may exist on the same property; each pays independently.
4. Duration is set at creation, measured in the property owner's passes of GO. Duration may be infinite. The countdown decrements when the owner passes GO.
5. The owner sets the initial price and sells the card for whatever the market will bear. Rebate cards are freely tradeable.
6. If the listed property changes hands, the rebate obligation follows the property ⚙ (the new owner pays; duration now counts the new owner's GO passes).

---

## 6. The Bank

1. The bank has infinite money. It never defaults on loans it must honor, insurance claims, or salary payments.
2. Houses and hotels remain limited to the standard physical supply (32 houses, 12 hotels).
3. (Planned variant, not v1: fixed money supply "hard mode.")

---

## 7. Order of Operations at GO

When a player passes GO, resolve in this exact order:

1. Collect $200 salary.
2. Pay loan interest — bank loans first, then player loans in order of card creation.
3. Pay insurance premiums (or let policies lapse); paid tranches refill to $500.
4. Cancellation window — insurance policies on this player may be canceled by either party.
5. Corporation distributions — if this player is a controlling interest, pay out accrued profits to all shareholders.
6. Rebate durations — decrement all rebate cards tied to properties this player owns.

If the player cannot meet an obligation at any step, forced liquidation begins immediately at that step (see §8).

---

## 8. Bankruptcy

### 8.1 Priority waterfall

When a player is liquidated, proceeds and assets satisfy obligations in strict priority:

1. Loans (bank loans first, then player loans by creation order)
2. Insurance obligations owed as an insurer
3. Rebate obligations owed
4. Corporate distributions owed

### 8.2 Resolution of cascades

1. Bankruptcies are resolved one at a time, in full, through the complete waterfall, before any other player's solvency is evaluated.
2. If resolving one bankruptcy renders other players insolvent, they are queued and resolved sequentially in turn order, starting from the current player.
3. Bankruptcy wipes out ownership: corporations controlled by the bankrupt player dissolve (§4.5), and their remaining cards transfer to (or are auctioned by) the creditor.

---

## 9. Winning

Last player standing wins. The game ends when all players but one are bankrupt.
